### Key Findings — Part A

1. **Extreme variety**: 11 different text types across 14 years. No single type dominates.
2. **"Letter" at 35% is over 14pp too high**: Combined formal letter + letter to editor = ~21% (3/14), not 35%.
3. **Missing types**: Photo captions (2013), yearbook entry (2019), application form (2023), promotional leaflet (2025).
4. **Blog, report, proposal, story, diary**: Listed at 3–10% but have **never appeared in actual Part A exams**.
5. **Email at 8% is reasonable** — 2024 was the first email Part A, but may become more common post-reform.
6. **Unusual/rare Part A types**: Photo captions, yearbook entry, application form — one-off appearances.

## Typical Genres for Each Text Type

### Article
- School magazine article (most common), Newspaper article, Feature article
- Newsletter article (company/school), Travel magazine article
- Music/entertainment magazine, Restaurant business article

### Letter (distinct sub-genres)
- **Letter to the Editor**: argumentative, formal, "Dear Editor" + "Yours faithfully"
- **Formal letter**: to principal/manager — persuasive or informative
- **Personal letter**: to friend/family — informal, "Dear + name" + "Best wishes"
- **Letter of advice**: semi-formal, to advice column reader
- **Letter of application**: formal, introducing qualifications
- **Parents' letter**: semi-formal (unique — 2018 Part A)

### Essay
- Argumentative essay (pro/con or for/against)
- Discursive/opinion essay
- Expository essay

### Speech
- School assembly speech (most common), Debate speech, Conference speech

### Email
- Formal email (to manager/teacher), Informal email (to friend/parents)
- Complaint email (2024 Part A)

### Other
- **Story**: Short story with scenario/prompt, creative writing
- **Blog**: Personal experience, music/entertainment, cultural trends, lifestyle
- **Report**: Survey/investigation with headings and sub-headings
- **Proposal**: Project/business with background, recommendations, expected outcomes
- **Review**: Film, restaurant, book review
- **Diary/Journal**: Personal reflective entry

### Unusual / One-off Types
- Photo captions (2013 Part A)
- Yearbook entry (2019 Part A)
- Application form/company profile (2023 Part A)
- Promotional leaflet (2025 Part A)
- Artist biography (2022 Part B Q6)
- Travel guide (2022 Part B Q1)
- Forum post (2012 Part B Q2, Q3 — none since)
- Information poster (Sample Paper Part A)
- News report (2020 Part B Q3)

## Answers to Original Questions

### Q1: Is the current distribution accurate?
**No.** Part A significantly overrepresents "letter" (35% vs ~21% actual) and includes types that have never appeared in Part A (blog, report, proposal, story, diary). Part B is broadly accurate but overweights report, speech, review, and includes "advertisement" which has never appeared.

### Q2: Most frequent text types in Part A vs Part B?
**Part A**: Article (14%), Speech (14%), Formal Letter (14%) — very evenly spread across 11 types in 14 years.
**Part B**: Article (~20%), Letter (~18%), Short Story (~10%).

### Q3: Unusual/rare text types?
**Part A**: Photo captions (2013), yearbook entry (2019), application form (2023), promotional leaflet (2025).
**Part B**: Artist biography (2022), travel guide (2022), forum post (2012 only), news report (2020).

### Q4: How many Part B options?
**2012–2023**: 8 questions (Q2–Q9), one per elective module.
**2024 onward**: 4 questions (Q2–Q5), electives delinked.

### Q5: Typical genre for each type?
See "Typical Genres for Each Text Type" section above. Key sub-genre distinctions: Letter to Editor ≠ Formal letter ≠ Personal letter ≠ Letter of advice ≠ Letter of application.

### Q6: Missing text types?
**From Part A**: Photo captions, yearbook entry, application form, promotional leaflet, visitor guide, information poster.
**From Part B**: Letter of advice (4 occurrences), Letter of application (2 occurrences), News Report (recount type). Advertisement has never appeared and should be removed.
