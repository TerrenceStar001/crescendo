# DSE English Paper 2 Writing — Prompt Phrasing & Structural Analysis

**Source:** Real past paper questions (2012–2024), HKEAA Assessment Framework, sample papers, and examiner reports.
**Research compiled from:** HKEAA official documents, Chenglish.hk, Spencer Lam English Team (spencerlam.hk), Sky for Excellence (skyforexcellence.com), DSE Treasure (dsetreasure.com), Young Post, icecreamtutor.com.

---

## 1. OVERALL EXAM STRUCTURE

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Time** | 2 hours (11:00 am – 1:00 pm) for both Parts |
| **Parts** | Part A (compulsory) + Part B (choose 1 question) |
| **Part A weight** | 10% of subject total |
| **Part B weight** | 15% of subject total |
| **Scoring** | Content 7 + Language 7 + Organisation 7 = 21 per part |
| **Conversion** | Part A score × 2, Part B score × 3 |

### Word Limits

- **Part A:** "For question 1, write about **200 words** in the space provided on pages 2 and 3."
- **Part B:** "For questions 2–5 [or 2–9 in older exams], choose ONE question and write about **400 words** in the space provided on pages 5–8."

### Candidate Identity Rules

> "Do not use your real name in answering any of the questions. If names are provided in the question, you must use those names. If no name is provided and you still wish to use a name to identify yourself, then use **'Chris Wong'**. If you need to use names for other characters in the composition not specified by the question, you may use names such as Mary, Peter, Mr Smith, Ms Young, etc. You may lose marks if you do not follow these instructions."

## 2. PART A vs PART B PHRASING DIFFERENCES

| Dimension | Part A | Part B |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Nature** | Short, guided writing task | Longer, more open-ended |
| **Word count** | ~200 words | ~400 words |
| **Number of choices** | 1 (compulsory) | 4 (2024+) or 8 (2012–2023) |
| **Scaffolding** | Heavy — headings, forms, diagrams, bullet points, partial text | Light — 1–2 sentence prompt, occasional bullet hints |
| **Text types** | Practical: email, form, guide, announcement, review, letter, diary, yearbook entry | Open: article, essay, speech, proposal, letter to editor, blog, report, story, feature article |
| **Role clarity** | Role always explicitly stated | Role stated but less directive |
| **Genre label** | No elective module label | Labeled by elective module (e.g., "Learning English through Debating") |
| **Max grade** | N/A (converted score) | B1 questions capped at Level 4; B2 questions can reach 5** |

## 3. PROMPT ANATOMY — The Standard Template

A DSE writing prompt typically follows this structure:

### Part A Pattern:
```
[ROLE]. [SITUATION/CONTEXT]. [TASK].
[Optional: provided materials like map/poster/form/headings].
[Sub-tasks listed as bullets or numbered points].
```

### Part B Pattern:
```
Learning English through [MODULE NAME]

[CONTEXT — 1-3 sentences establishing scenario/issue]
[TASK VERB — Write a [text type] for [audience/purpose]]
[Sub-task bullets — optional, 2-4 bullet points]
[Optional role phrase: "Sign your name as Chris Wong"]
```

## 4. ROLE ASSIGNMENT PATTERNS

Roles fall into these consistent categories:

### Student/School Roles (most common)
- "You are a student leader at your school."
- "You are a member of the Students' Association."
- "You are the chairperson of the Social Service Club in your school."
- "You are the President of the Students' Union at your school."
- "You are a student in Class 6C at Kowloon Secondary School."
- "You are the captain of the school's basketball team."
- "You are a school reporter."
- "You are the host of 'Teen Chat', a YouTube channel..."
- "You are taking part in a student-organised exhibition..."

### Professional/Workplace Roles
- "You work at the Visitor Information Centre at Hong Kong Adventure Farm."
- "You are an employee for Planet Smart, a company selling eco-friendly goods."
- "You write an advice column for Jobs Online magazine."
- "You are a reporter for the sports section of the Young Post."
- "You are a famous news reporter for Hong Kong News."
- "You are a human resources assistant."
- "You are an intern at Hong Kong Correctional Services."
- "You are a blogger who writes about cultural trends."
- "You are a successful musician and a writer of a music blog."

### Public Citizen / Advocate Roles
- "You strongly believe that more land should be used for open space and recreation."
- "You are the chairperson of your school's Heritage Club."
- "You would like to propose Dragon Boat Racing [to the IOC]."

### Creative/Character Roles
- "You are the lead singer in a band."
- "Imagine you are a pet bird in a cage."
- "You are a famous news reporter for Hong Kong News."
- "You played the role of the hero and the villain in different plays."

## 5. TASK VERBS — Complete Inventory

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Write** | "Write the article." / "Write a letter to the editor." / "Write a proposal." / "Write a blog post." / "Write an essay." / "Write a speech." / "Write a story." / "Write a diary entry." / "Write a news article." |
| **Explain** | "Explain why you were disappointed." / "Explain the reasons." / "Explain why sales of electric vehicles are so low." / "Explain this trend." |
| **Describe** | "Describe two activities." / "Describe some of the activities." / "Describe the incident." / "Describe the performance." |
| **Discuss** | "Discuss whether social media influencers are more trustworthy." / "Discuss both sides of the issue." / "Discuss what a critic does." |
| **Suggest** | "Suggest two ways to use the fund." / "Suggest what could be done." / "Suggest what changes could be made." |
| **Give reasons** | "Give reasons to support your view." / "Give reasons to support your suggestion." |
| **State your opinion** | "State your opinion either for or against a four-day school week." |
| **Argue** | "Argue either for OR against the effectiveness of food warning labels." |
| **Reflect** | "Reflect on which role you preferred playing." / "Reflect on the 21 days." |
| **Introduce** | "Introduce the characters, plot and setting." / "Introduce yourself and your athletic history." |
| **Persuade** | "Persuade your principal to accept your project." |
| **Justify** | "Justify your position with three reasons." |
| **Propose** | "Write a proposal." (implied propose) / "Suggest two new activities." |
| **Recommend** | "Recommend changes." / "Give three recommendations." |
| **Summarise** | "Summarise details of a recent complaint." |
| **Highlight** | "Highlight your work experience." |
| **Choose one side** | "Choose one side of the argument and state your position." |
| **Outline** | "Outline a product that your consumers love." / "Outline possible solutions." |

**Compound Task Pattern:** Most questions combine 2-3 verbs:
> "Explain why you started this channel **and** what you have learned in the process."
> "Describe one activity that could be carried out, **and** identifying the benefits for the elderly home."

## 6. INSTRUCTION STYLE — Exact Patterns Found

### Name Instructions
"Sign your letter Chris Wong." / "Sign your name as Chris Wong."

### Module Labels (2012–2023 format, used consistently)
Each Part B question is prefixed with:
```
Learning English through Sports Communication
Learning English through Drama
Learning English through Poems and Songs
Learning English through Debating
Learning English through Popular Culture
Learning English through Short Stories
Learning English through Workplace Communication
Learning English through Social Issues
```

### Text Type Directives
"Write a letter to the editor..." / "Write an article for Teen Magazine..." / "Write a blog post..." / "Write an argumentative essay..."

### Heading Instructions (Part A)
"Use the following three headings to write the article. Add one heading of your own."
"Use the map and headings below to help you complete a guide..."

### Bullet Point Hints
Standard format — 2-4 bullet points following the task, introduced with:
```
In your email, you should explain:
• why you were disappointed; and
• what you would like the manager to do.
```

### "You may use" / "You can use"
"You can choose to write about any of the following in your review." / "You may use the mindmap to help you write your letter." / "You can use the pictures in any order."

### Part A Signature Lines
Some Part A questions have signature lines pre-printed (e.g., a form with blank fields).

## 7. SCAFFOLDING LEVELS

### Part A — Heavy Scaffolding (3 types observed):

**Type 1 — Form/Template with Headings:**
> (2024 Sample) "Complete the following questionnaire after taking the class." with headings: *Why did you want to take this course? / What did you like best about this course? / What changes would you recommend?*

**Type 2 — Pre-written Start:**
> (2016 Part A) "Good morning Principal, teachers and fellow students, On behalf of the Students' Union, I'd like to welcome all of you to our school..." → Candidates finish the speech.
> (2015 Part A) "Dear Editor, ..." → Some parts given, candidate completes.

**Type 3 — Visual/Map + Headings:**
> (2022 Part A) Map of Hong Kong Adventure Farm + headings: *About Hong Kong Adventure Farm / Top three things to see and do*

**Type 4 — Just Bullet Points (most common):**
> (2024 Part A) "In your email, you should explain: • why you were disappointed; and • what you would like the manager to do."

### Part B — Light Scaffolding

Recent years (2020–2024): 1-2 sentence context statement + 2-4 bullet points.
Older years (2012–2019): More directive, often with:
- Specific number of reasons required ("three reasons")
- Pre-set headings to use
- Images/pictures to incorporate
- More detailed bullet point lists

Example of minimum scaffolding (2024 Sample Paper):
> **Question 4:** "You are a blogger who writes about cultural trends. Write a blog post on how Hong Kong popular culture has been influenced by Japan and/or Korea."
> → Only 2 sentences, no bullet points, no sub-tasks listed.

Example of heavier scaffolding (2012):
> **Question 7:** "You were walking by the Peninsula Hotel in Tsim Sha Tsui when you spotted your brother driving an expensive sports car. The last time you spoke to him, he was looking for a job. Write a story about how you found out the truth."
> → Image provided + detailed scenario.

## 8. WORD LIMIT SPECIFICATION

- **Part A:** "For question 1, write about **200 words**."
- **Part B:** "For questions 2–5, choose ONE question and write about **400 words**."
- The phrase "about" is always used — not a strict limit.
- The HKEAA examiner report states: "Longer responses will not necessarily gain higher marks simply by virtue of length."
- Answer space is provided on the question paper (ruled pages), which implicitly guides length.
- Supplementary answer sheets are available on request.

## 9. AUTHENTIC PROMPTS — Exact Wording

### 2024 Actual Exam — Part A
> After seeing the advertisement below, you booked a one-night stay at the Seaside Hotel. However, you were not happy with your stay because the room was very different from what was shown on the hotel's website.
> Write an email to the hotel manager. In your email, you should explain:
> • why you were disappointed; and
> • what you would like the manager to do.

### 2024 Actual Exam — Part B (4-option format)
> **Question 2:** A century ago, women in Hong Kong had on average five children. Today, that number is less than one. Write an essay which gives reasons why having fewer children is more desirable now than in the past.
>
> **Question 3:** You are a successful musician and a writer of a music blog. A reader recently recorded a song and has asked you how to promote the song. Write a blog post on how musicians can get more people to listen to their music.
>
> **Question 4:** The Leisure and Cultural Services Department (LCSD) has designated some parks and promenades as 'Dog-Friendly' places. However, some people are not happy about this new trend. Write a letter to the editor of the Hong Kong Post expressing your views.
>
> **Question 5:** You posted a video on social media that went viral and gained 100,000 likes overnight. Write an article for the school magazine about your experience.

### 2024 Sample Paper — Part A
> Last month, you took part in one of the classes offered at the Summer Institute. The Institute would like you to complete the following questionnaire after taking the class. [Questionnaire form with: Why did you want to take this course? / What did you like best about this course? / What changes would you recommend to improve this course?]

### 2024 Sample Paper — Part B
> **Question 2:** You are taking part in a student-organised exhibition on 'What makes Hong Kong Special'. You would like to write an article about the Star Ferry and have interviewed two people for your article, which will be posted on the exhibition's website. Write the article.
>
> **Question 3:** Your school has been awarded a government fund. You are a member of the Students' Association. Write a proposal to the School Management suggesting two ways to use the fund to improve the facilities and/or learning and teaching in the school.
>
> **Question 4:** You are a blogger who writes about cultural trends. Write a blog post on how Hong Kong popular culture has been influenced by Japan and/or Korea.
>
> **Question 5:** A significant proportion of Hong Kong land is developed for residential or commercial purposes. You strongly believe that more land should be used for open space and recreation. The Town Planning Board is inviting the public to express their views. Write a letter to the board and support your arguments with reasons.

### 2023 Actual Exam — Part A
> You are an employee for Planet Smart, a company selling eco-friendly goods. Your team wants to bid for a pop-up space in a shopping mall for 5 months for the purpose of promoting your products to new customers. Complete the application form below.
> A. Company Profile (introduction)
> B. Best-selling Product (outline a product that your consumers love)
> C. In-store Event (describe a marketing event that will be held in store to attract customers)

### 2023 Actual Exam — Part B
> **Question 2 (Sports):** While tai chi is a popular activity in Hong Kong, it is less known in some parts of the world. Write an article for International Travel magazine introducing the benefits of tai chi to tourists.
>
> **Question 4 (Debating):** The following topic was discussed at a debate workshop: Some employers use cameras and computer software to monitor what employees are doing in the workplace. How do you think this affects productivity and job satisfaction? Write an essay about the pros and cons of monitoring employees in the workplace.
>
> **Question 7 (Social Issues):** A survey found that the majority of Hong Kong people in their 60s would like to have the option to work past the retirement age of 65. In contrast, there is a growing number of young people whose goal is to retire in their 30s or 40s. Write a news article for the Hong Kong Daily about these different views towards retirement.
>
> **Question 8 (Poems and Songs):** Contestants in a song-writing competition complained when a song created by artificial intelligence (AI) software won first prize. Write a letter to the editor on whether computer-generated songs should be allowed to compete in such competitions.

### 2022 Actual Exam — Part A
> You work at the Visitor Information Centre at Hong Kong Adventure Farm. Use the map and headings below to help you complete a guide for first-time visitors.
> • About Hong Kong Adventure Farm
> • Top three things to see and do

### 2022 Actual Exam — Part B
> **Question 2 (Popular Culture):** You are the host of 'Teen Chat', a YouTube channel that explores a wide range of topics from a teenage perspective. Write an article for Teen Magazine about why you started this channel and what you have learned in the process.
>
> **Question 3 (Workplace Communication):** You write an advice column for Jobs Online magazine. A reader submitted the following question: "My dream is to open a café with a few friends, but I've just been offered a promotion at my company. The pay is good, but I'm not that interested in the work. Should I leave now or wait a few years?" Write a reply to Kam Chai offering your advice.
>
> **Question 4 (Sports):** You are the captain of the school's basketball team. Your team made it to the inter-school championship final but came second. Write a speech to be delivered to the team at the end-of-season dinner celebration.
>
> **Question 9 (Debating):** The School Management is considering a four-day school week. Students would only need to attend school four days a week instead of five, but the length of each day would be increased by one hour. You are a member of the Parent-Teacher Association (PTA). Write a letter to the President of the PTA stating your opinion either for or against a four-day school week. Give reasons to support your view.

### 2012 Actual Exam — Part A
> You are a famous news reporter for Hong Kong News. Your old secondary school has asked you to write a feature article about yourself for the next edition of the school magazine. Use the following three headings to write the article. Add one heading of your own.
> • The best part of my job
> • The biggest challenge I have ever faced in my job
> • My school days

## 10. DURATION OF PHRASING PATTERNS

The following patterns have remained consistent for **13+ years** (2012–2025):

1. **"Write about X words"** — unchanged phrasing since 2012
2. **"You are a [role]."** — the standard role introduction
3. **"Write a [text type] for [audience]."** — the standard task directive
4. **"In your [text type], you should [task verb]"** — introduces sub-tasks
5. **"Sign your name as Chris Wong."** / "Sign your letter Chris Wong."
6. **"Give reasons to support your view."** — standard closure instruction
7. **Part B module labels** ("Learning English through...") — used from 2012–2023, discontinued 2024+
8. **Bullet points** for sub-tasks — consistent across all years
9. **"You may lose marks if you do not follow these instructions."** — standard warning

### Changes in 2024+
- Part B reduced from 8 questions to 4 questions
- Module labels ("Learning English through X") removed from question headers
- Questions became more self-contained (less reliance on elective module knowledge)
- Scaffolding reduced further — some questions have no bullet points at all

## 11. KEY TERMINOLOGY IN EXAMINER INSTRUCTION

From the HKEAA Assessment Framework and Examiner Reports:

> "Candidates should attempt Part A and ONE question from Part B."
> "For Part B, you should put an 'X' in the corresponding question number box."
> "Answers written in the margins will not be marked."
> "Rough work should be done on the rough-work sheets which will be collected separately. These will not be marked."
> "No extra time will be given to candidates for sticking on the barcode labels or filling in the question number boxes after the 'Time is up' announcement."
